Attribute Directive

  • Attribute directives in Angular are used to modify the behavior or appearance of an element, component, or another directive. Unlike structural directives, which change the structure of the DOM, attribute directives only affect the element they are applied to.
  • Key Points
    1. Purpose: Attribute directives modify the appearance or behavior of DOM elements.
    2. Usage: Typically applied as an attribute to an element.
    3. HostListener and HostBinding: Used in custom directives to respond to events and bind properties.
  • Some build in attribute directive are
    1. ngClass
    2. ngStyle
    3. ngModel
